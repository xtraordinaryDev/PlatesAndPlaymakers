THE STORY

FROM 1992
TO TODAY.

It started in 1992 as Taste of the Tours — a beloved Twin Cities tradition that brought neighbors together around great food and a greater cause. For more than three decades, these events celebrated the chefs and kitchens that define this region while channeling the energy of a night out into something that lasts: over 100 million meals created for people across the metro who needed them most.

Plates & Playmakers is the next chapter. This rebrand is a deliberate act — a centralizing of purpose to tackle hunger in the Twin Cities head-on. Same spirit. Bigger mission. More focused than ever on the neighborhoods, food shelves, and families that need us most right now.

Events are organized by Hunger Related Events — the 501(c)(3) nonprofit that has put on hunger-relief dining events across the Twin Cities for decades, and whose archive of past events shows just how far this community has come.

WHERE THE MONEY GOES

THREE FOOD BANKS.
ONE TWIN CITIES.

The bulk of every dollar raised goes directly to three food bank partners doing essential work across Minneapolis and Saint Paul — covering bulk supply, neighborhood food shelves, and rapid-response meal distribution. A dedicated portion also supports independent local restaurants and hospitality businesses so small kitchens can keep serving their communities.

EVERY MEAL

Every Meal fights child hunger in Minnesota by providing weekend food bags and meals to kids who might otherwise go without. Their programs reach children across the metro directly through school and community partnerships, ensuring no child faces the week on an empty stomach.

VISIT EVERYMEAL.ORG →
ICA FOOD SHELF

The ICA Food Shelf has served the western suburbs of Minneapolis for decades, providing groceries, produce, and essential household items to neighbors in need. Their neighborhood-rooted model means people can access real food — with dignity — close to home.

VISIT ICAFOODSHELF.ORG →
NOURISH MN

Nourish MN — formerly known as the Emergency Foodshelf Network — is the backbone of food shelf support across the state. They connect and strengthen Minnesota's network of food shelves, provide rapid emergency response, and advocate for policies that address the root causes of hunger.

VISIT NOURISHMN.ORG →
LOCAL RESTAURANTS
& HOSPITALITY

A dedicated portion of proceeds supports the independent restaurants and hospitality businesses that feed, employ, and anchor Twin Cities neighborhoods. Small kitchens operate on thin margins — a targeted investment helps them stay open, stay staffed, and keep showing up for their blocks.

THE ORGANIZER

Hunger Related Events is the 501(c)(3) nonprofit behind Plates & Playmakers — and the organization that has been putting on hunger-relief dining events across the Twin Cities since 1992. From the original Taste of the Tours gatherings to today, they have connected great chefs, generous guests, and hungry neighbors in a tradition that has now produced over 100 million meals.

Their archive of past events tells the full story of what this community has built together — and where Plates & Playmakers fits within it.

INDUSTRY SUPPORT

THE SALT
CURE FUND.

Our primary mission is hunger relief across the Twin Cities. But we also recognize the people who feed us — the cooks, servers, dishwashers, and kitchen workers who power this city's food culture and often face their own crises with little safety net beneath them.

A portion of proceeds supports The Salt Cure Fund, which provides direct financial assistance to culinary industry workers in times of illness, injury, or hardship. Because the people who make great food deserve to be cared for too.
