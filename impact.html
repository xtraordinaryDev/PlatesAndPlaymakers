THE STORY

FROM 1992
TO TODAY.

It started in 1992 as Taste of the NFL — the premier charity event of Super Bowl weekend, put on by the team at Hunger Related Events. Over three decades, the tour expanded into Taste of the Masters, Taste of the Derby, and a roster of other signature events around the country — generating over 100 million meals for families in need along the way.

Plates & Playmakers is the next chapter — and it's more focused on Minnesota than ever. After three decades of events around the country, we're channeling that energy into one unforgettable Minneapolis night that brings together the best Minnesota has to offer: our great chefs, our beloved athletes, and our most generous neighbors, all in service of the food shelves and families that need us most.

Events are organized by Hunger Related Events — the 501(c)(3) nonprofit whose archive of past events shows just how far this work has come.

OUR PARTNERS

THREE FOOD BANKS.
ONE MINNESOTA.

Plates & Playmakers supports a coalition tackling hunger across Minnesota — three remarkable food bank partners doing essential work in bulk supply, neighborhood food shelves, and rapid-response meal distribution. We also stand behind the people who feed us through The Salt Cure Fund, providing direct support to culinary industry workers in times of need.

EVERY MEAL

Every Meal fights child hunger in Minnesota by providing weekend food bags and meals to kids who might otherwise go without. Their programs reach children across the metro directly through school and community partnerships, ensuring no child faces the week on an empty stomach.

VISIT EVERYMEAL.ORG →
ICA FOOD SHELF

The ICA Food Shelf has served the western suburbs of Minneapolis for decades, providing groceries, produce, and essential household items to neighbors in need. Their neighborhood-rooted model means people can access real food — with dignity — close to home.

VISIT ICAFOODSHELF.ORG →
THE FOOD GROUP

The Food Group is the backbone of food shelf support across Minnesota. They connect and strengthen the state's network of food shelves, run rapid emergency response, distribute culturally-relevant fresh produce, and advocate for the policies that address the root causes of hunger.

VISIT THEFOODGROUPMN.ORG →
THE SALT CURE FUND

The Salt Cure Fund provides direct financial assistance to culinary industry workers — the cooks, servers, dishwashers, and kitchen staff who power Minnesota's food culture — in times of illness, injury, or hardship. Because the people who make great food deserve to be cared for too.
